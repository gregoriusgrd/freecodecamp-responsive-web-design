Nelson Mandela

The Man Who Fought for Freedom and Equality

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

Early Life

  • 1918 - Born on July 18 in Mvezo, South Africa, as Rolihlahla Mandela.
  • 1925 - Began attending a local Methodist school, where he was given the English name "Nelson."
  • 1939 - Enrolled at the University of Fort Hare, South Africa's first higher education institute for black students.
  • 1940 - Expelled from Fort Hare for participating in a student protest.

Early Activism

  • 1941 - Moved to Johannesburg; worked as a night watchman and later as a clerk in a law firm.
  • 1943 - Joined the African National Congress (ANC) and became involved in anti-apartheid activism.
  • 1944 - Co-founded the ANC Youth League to energize the struggle against apartheid.

Political Struggles

  • 1952 - Opened South Africa’s first black law firm with Oliver Tambo. Led the Defiance Campaign, the first large-scale anti-apartheid movement.
  • 1956 - Arrested and charged with treason alongside 155 other activists; acquitted in 1961.

Imprisonment

  • 1961 - Became a leader of the armed wing of the ANC, Umkhonto we Sizwe.
  • 1962 - Arrested and sentenced to five years in prison for inciting strikes and leaving the country without a passport.
  • 1964 - Sentenced to life imprisonment during the Rivonia Trial for sabotage and conspiracy.

Freedom and Leadership

  • 1990 - Released from prison on February 11 after 27 years. His release was broadcast worldwide.
  • 1991 - Elected president of the ANC. Began negotiations to end apartheid.
  • 1993 - Won the Nobel Peace Prize with F.W. de Klerk for efforts to dismantle apartheid.
  • 1994 - Became South Africa’s first black president after the country’s first democratic election.

Later Years

  • 1999 - Stepped down as president after one term but continued global advocacy for peace and human rights.
  • 2001 - Diagnosed with prostate cancer.
  • 2004 - Retired from public life, stating, "Don't call me, I'll call you."
  • 2013 - Passed away on December 5 in Johannesburg at the age of 95.
Mandela's life and legacy stand as a testament to the enduring power of resilience, compassion, and the unwavering pursuit of justice for all.

— Former President Barack Obama
